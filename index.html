Air-water heat pumps
Hajster NEBO split

Air-to-water heat pumps use air as a heat source, making them the simplest to install. They do not require interaction with the ground or groundwater. The technology provides three functions at once: heating, cooling, and hot water heating. The execution is "split" - the external and internal units are connected by a refrigerant line.

  • COP (coefficient of performance) - 5.05
  • Operating in winter to - 25°C, operating in summer to +50°C
  • Maximum heat carrier supply temperature +62°C
  • Refrigerant R32
  • Modern controller of our own development and web access
  • Warranty - 5 years

We have considered the technology in more detail in our article "Air heat pump split system".

Air-water heat pumps
Hajster NEBO mono

The air-to-water heat pump uses air as a heat source, making it the simplest to install as it requires no interaction with water or the ground. This technology serves three functions simultaneously: heating, cooling, and hot water supply. The "monoblock" design integrates external and internal units through a hydraulic line.

  • COP (Coefficient of Performance): 5.03
  • Winter operation down to -25°C, summer operation up to +50°C
  • Maximum supply temperature of the heat transfer fluid: +75°C
  • Refrigerant: R290
  • Advanced in-house developed controller with web access
  • Warranty: 5 year

We have considered the technology in more detail in our article "Monoblock air source heat pump".

Geothermal heat pumps
Hajster GRUNT

An inverter geothermal heat pump uses the heat of the earth or groundwater as a heat source and provides three functions at once: heating, cooling, and hot water heating.

  • COP (coefficient of performance) - 6.2
  • Operation in winter down to -25°C, operation in summer up to +50°C
  • Maximum coolant supply temperature +60°C
  • R32 refrigerant
  • Unrivaled noise and vibration insulation "acoustic shell" technology
  • Modern controller of own design and web access
  • Warranty - 5 years

We have considered the technology in more detail in our article "Geothermal heat pump overview".

Meet the Hajster
The Hajster, also known as the stork or the black stork, is a bird that is a symbol of Ukraine. In search of a comfortable climate, it travels between Ukraine and Africa, including the Sahara.

Hajster is an innovative startup of the "Sahara" company, which was founded in 2000 and was one of the first to offer advanced European and global energy-efficient technologies based on renewable energy sources for the Ukrainian market.

Today, the company has 23 years of experience in the design and installation of climate systems, and all this potential has been invested in the development of the innovative Ukrainian heat pump - Hajster.

The development and production of Hajster heat pumps use the best global components and are 100% located in Ukraine.
